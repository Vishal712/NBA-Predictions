The project we've chosen is about the NBA, and how specific stats can determine how efficient and impactful someone is in the leage. Throughout the history of the NBA, players have been always able to record massive numbers, and yet aren't considered the same level as the top players, such as Michael Jordan, Patrick Ewing, Kevin Durant. Why is that? What makes a player just a starter, compared to all stars? What about MVP candidates? What kind of stats and production is needed for that?

The way we plan on answering these questions is through machine learning, specifically through 3 models. The end goal of these models is to find a way to predict the efficiency and impact of a player based on given stats. And to do this, we used decades of data. This dataset from Kaggle(https://www.kaggle.com/drgilermo/nba-players-stats) contains player data from the 1950s, for each player in each season. By including these stats, we are able to train 3 different models, and output a classification of PER, or player efficiency rating.
